Hi there!

I’m Medha Agarwal, a fourth-year undergraduate in the Department of Mathematics and Statistics at IIT Kanpur. I grew up in a district called Meerut (very close to New Delhi) and am currently pursuing my undergraduate studies in Kanpur. My love for mathematics brought me to this wonderful department at IITK, where I developed a keen interest in statistics right from my first introductory course.

Since my sophomore year, I have explored different topics in statistics through research projects and courses. This has left me fascinated, inquisitive about further possibilities, and most importantly, eager to learn more. I have learned a lot in Markov chain Monte Carlo through my project under Prof. Dootika Vats, which has led to a paper, now available on arxiv. I have also enjoyed a different but equally captivating field in mathematics -numerical optimization algorithms (MM algorithm and Broyden’s method) through my project under Prof. Jason Xu. I am currently working on an exciting problem in importance sampling under Prof. Dootika Vats and Prof. Victor Elvira.

While at IITK, I have had the opportunity of being mentored by an incredible professor, make great friends, and drink good coffee. It has been a delightful journey (sadly, coming to an end soon) at IITK. I look forward to richer experiences from graduate school. Apart from research, I love to dance, read fiction, write free verse poetry, drink dark roast coffee, and travel to the mountains. I have also received formal training in Indian classical dance form Kathak from Allahabad University and graduated first division :)
